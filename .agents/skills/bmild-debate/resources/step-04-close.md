## Close — Session Conclusion

Close the session briefly — the synthesis already did the heavy work. Each Lead makes an in-character closing remark. Do not produce a long wrap-up.

1. **Session outcome** — Facilitator states in one sentence what the session produced:

   > *"This session established [key outcome — e.g. the API contract approach, the deferred UX question, the confirmed non-negotiable]."*

2. **Lead sign-offs** — Two or three Leads who were most engaged in the session make brief, in-character closing remarks (two sentences maximum):

   ```
   🟦 Faisal: [brief closing — pragmatic, user-focused]

   🟩 Katrina: [brief closing — interaction or design-focused]

   🟥 Lance: [brief closing — concrete, constraint-aware]

   🟨 Rahat: [brief closing — risk or edge-case-aware]
   ```

   Example beats: Faisal — *"Good session. Let's make sure the open item on [X] gets resolved before Sonia starts slicing — it'll block her otherwise."* Katrina — *"The interaction model is clearer now. I'd want to revisit the error-state flow once Lance's API shape is confirmed."* Lance — *"The schema decision is solid. Watch the index on [column] at volume."* Rahat — *"Make sure [edge case] gets a test. It'll come back if it doesn't."*

3. **Return handoff** — Facilitator tells the user where to continue:

   > *"debate session closed. [If invoked from within a persona session:] You were working with [persona name] on [feature/topic]. Resume there when ready. [If invoked directly:] Ready for your next BMILD persona whenever you are."*

Natural close phrases that route here: `done`, `exit`, `close session`, `that's enough`, `wrap up`. The `[E]` option at the end of each debate round also routes here.
